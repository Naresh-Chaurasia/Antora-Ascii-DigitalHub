== Practice Partner Tips

=== Set the Right Frame from the Start

You're a practice partner, not her personal instructor. Make that clear — not verbally in a harsh way, but through how you structure the sessions. Come with a plan of what *you* want to work on too.

=== Have Your Own Agenda

Before each session, decide 1–2 things you personally want to drill — footwork, leading clarity, musicality, timing. Don't just show up to "help her." You're also there to improve yourself.

=== Teach, but Don't Over-Explain

Since she's new, she'll naturally look to you for guidance. That's actually great for you — teaching reinforces your own understanding. But keep it simple, correct one thing at a time, and don't turn into a full-time coach.

=== Keep Sessions Time-Bounded

Agree on a fixed duration — say 1 to 1.5 hours. This keeps it professional and prevents it from becoming a social hangout disguised as practice.

=== Stay Emotionally Neutral

This is the big one. Don't invest emotionally in how she responds to your teaching — whether she's grateful, distracted, or slow to pick up. Detach your self-worth from her reactions.

=== Evaluate After 2–3 Sessions

Ask yourself honestly — am I growing from this? Is she serious about improving? If it feels one-sided, it's okay to scale back or stop.

[NOTE]
====
The mindset to carry in: *you're two people using each other's presence to get better.* That's it. Respect, mutual effort, clear boundaries.
====

== Dealing with the Tendency to Give Too Much

=== The Core Pattern to Understand

When someone has a tendency to "give too much," it usually comes from one of these places:

* Wanting to be liked or approved of
* Fear that if you don't over-give, they'll lose interest
* Tying your value to how much you can do for someone else

The dance context makes this even more tricky — because _helping_ feels justified. "I'm just being a good partner." But there's a difference between genuine generosity and giving from a place of neediness.

=== What "Giving Too Much" Actually Costs You

* You stop being an equal and become a resource
* *She stops seeing you as someone to respect and starts seeing you as someone to use (often without even realizing it)*
* You end up resentful, and she ends up indifferent

=== The Shift in Mindset

Stop thinking _"what can I give her"_ and start thinking _"is this exchange working for both of us."_

Your time, energy, and knowledge have value. Treat them that way.

=== One Practical Rule

Match her effort:

[cols="1,1", options="header"]
|===
| Her Energy Level
| Your Response

| Serious and consistent practice energy
| Give fully

| Casual or flaky
| Pull back proportionally
|===

Simple mirror principle.

=== The Bigger Picture

This dance situation is actually a good low-stakes place to *practice* this new behavior — not just dance, but how you show up with people in general.
