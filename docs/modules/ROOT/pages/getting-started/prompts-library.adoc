=== Content Creation: Convert Text to AsciiDoc


.Convert the following text into clean AsciiDoc format
[%collapsible]
====

[source,asciidoc]
----
Convert the following text into clean AsciiDoc format.

Extract the key ideas from the following text and convert them into a structured bullet-point summary.

*Instructions:*

- Identify the main concepts and supporting ideas.
- Organize the summary hierarchically.
- Use bullet levels to represent the structure of ideas.

*Formatting rules:*

- Use the following bullet hierarchy:
  * Main idea
  ** Sub idea
  *** Deeper explanation or example
- Each bullet should contain one clear idea.
- Keep sentences short and easy to read.
- Remove filler text, repetition, and conversational phrases.
- Preserve important examples or analogies if they help understanding.
- Maintain logical grouping of ideas.

Output requirements:
- Output only the bullet summary.
- Ensure the format is clean so it can be copied with one click and pasted into notes or documentation tools.

== Dance

.Deep, thoughtful, and structured discussion about dance as an art form
[%collapsible]
====

[source,asciidoc]
----
I want to have a deep, thoughtful, and structured discussion about dance as an art form.

Treat dance not just as movement, but as a form of expression, communication, and identity.

Guide the discussion step-by-step using thought-provoking questions, reflections, and insights. Push me to think deeper rather than giving surface-level answers.

Focus on areas like:

* Dance as self-expression vs performance
* The role of musicality and emotion in dance
* Connection (with self, partner, and audience)
* Technique vs artistry
* Improvisation vs choreography
* Masculine and feminine energy in dance
* Presence, confidence, and storytelling through movement
* The difference between social dancing and performing
* What makes a dancer “memorable” vs just “good”

Also:

* Challenge my assumptions when needed
* Ask follow-up questions based on my responses
* Bring examples from styles like Salsa and Bachata where relevant
* Help me connect these ideas to my own dance journey (practice, teaching, performing)

Start with one deep question and then continue the discussion interactively based on my responses.
