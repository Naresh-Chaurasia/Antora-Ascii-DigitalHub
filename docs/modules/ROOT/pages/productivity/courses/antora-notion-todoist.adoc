This page defines where different kinds of work should live.

My system has **three clearly separated workspaces**:

* Notion — thinking and capture
* Todoist — planning and tracking (execution)
* Antora (AsciiDoc) — knowledge and documentation

Each has a clear responsibility.

== The Three Workspaces

=== 1. Notion — Thinking & Capture

Use Notion when the content is:

* Fast
* Personal
* Unstructured
* Exploratory
* Contextual or emotional

Typical examples:

* Quick ideas
* Brain dumps
* Rough notes
* Draft thoughts
* Journaling
* Explorations

Notion is a *cognitive scratchpad*.
It is **not** a knowledge base.

---

=== 2. Todoist — Planning & Tracking (Execution)

Planning and tracking are neither thinking nor knowledge.
They are about *execution*.

Use Todoist when the content is:

* Time-bound
* Actionable
* Status-driven
* Continuously changing
* Measured by completion

Typical examples:

* Tasks
* Projects
* Goals
* Habits
* Learning plans with deadlines
* Reviews (weekly or monthly)

Todoist answers the question:

*What should I do next?*

Tasks never become documents.
If a task needs explanation, the explanation lives elsewhere.

---

=== 3. Antora (AsciiDoc) — Knowledge & Documentation

Use Antora when the content is:

* Long-lived
* Structured
* Teachable
* Referential
* Reusable

Typical examples:

* Canonical notes
* Teaching material
* Architecture documentation
* Integration patterns
* Learning paths
* Principles and systems
* Decision records

There is **no standalone AsciiDoc**.
All AsciiDoc is published via Antora.

Antora answers the question:

*What do I know?*

== Diagram Policy (Antora‑First)

All diagrams are published via Antora, so stability matters.

*Decision and thinking diagrams*
* Plain text or simple Mermaid

*Teaching explanations*
* Mermaid is acceptable
* Keep syntax ASCII-only and simple

*Architecture and systems*
* Prefer PlantUML (via Kroki)
* Deterministic and long-term stable

Mermaid is a *visual aid*, not a source of truth.

== Practical Rules

* Fast and messy → Notion
* Time-bound and status-driven → Todoist
* Clear and teachable → Antora
* If it must survive 6–12 months → Antora
* If progress matters more than insight → Todoist

== One-Line System Definition

Notion is for thinking.  
Todoist is for execution.  
Antora is for knowledge.

This separation keeps the system clean, scalable, and low-friction over time.
