= Anki for Learning

> This is Anki entry point. Coding, Ideas etc.

== ✅ Minimal Starter Setup (Recommended for First 2 Weeks)

Use ONE main deck and organise people by *context*.

[source]
----
📂 People & Names
  ├── 💼 Work
  ├── 💃 Salsa
  ├── 🕺 Bachata
  └── 📸 Instagram
----

* This structure is:
** Easy to maintain
** *Matches how your brain recalls people*
** Scales naturally as you meet more people

---

== 💼 Work

[source]
----
📂 Work
----
Use this for:

* Cognizant colleagues
* Clients
* Professional contacts

(You can split later into subfolders like *Close Collaborators* or *Leadership* if needed.)

---

== 💃 Dance – Salsa


Include:

* Instructors
* Regular partners
* Social dancers you meet often

Card context examples:

* Batch / day
* Partner role (Lead / Follow)
* Any memorable dance cue

---

== 🕺 Dance – Bachata


Include:

* Bachata-only friends
* Advanced / social dancers
* Friends you mainly meet on the dance floor

Avoid duplicates:
If someone is both Instagram + Bachata, keep them here.

---

== 📸 Instagram Friends


Include:

* Friends you mainly know online
* College / travel / social circle
* Light‑context connections

Keep cards simple:

* Photo
* Name
* One short context line

---

== 🧠 Golden Rules

* **1 Person = 1 Note**
* One note can generate **multiple cards**
* #Organise by *where you know them from*, not importance#
* Start simple — refine later
* Avoid alphabetical or over‑detailed folders early

---

== ✅ Final Advice

Start with just these four folders:

[source]
----
People & Names
  ├── Work
  ├── Salsa
  ├── Bachata
  └── Instagram
----

Once the habit sticks, optimise further.

== ✅ Key Reminder

* You are **NOT writing biographies**
* Aim for **instant recognition**, not completeness
* If card creation feels heavy → simplify the back

Rule of thumb:
Name + Context + 1 Cue = Perfect card ✅
