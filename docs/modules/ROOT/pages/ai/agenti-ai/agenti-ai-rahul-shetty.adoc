=== 3. Transition from LLM -> AI Agents. Where AI Agent shines?


* Transition from LLMs to AI Agents
** LLMs mainly generate text responses based on training data.
** AI agents go beyond text generation and can perform real-world or system actions.

* Limitation of LLMs
** LLMs respond based on patterns learned from massive training datasets.
** They cannot directly perform actions like:
*** Connecting to a database
*** Automating a browser
*** Calling backend APIs
*** Editing files or code
** They can only explain *how* to do these tasks, not execute them.

* What AI Agents Do
** AI agents can perform actions on behalf of the user.
** They combine:
*** LLM intelligence (the brain)
*** Tools and integrations (hands and legs)
** This allows them to:
*** Access systems
*** Retrieve data
*** Perform tasks automatically

* Passport Application Analogy
** Using an LLM
*** You ask how to apply for a passport.
*** The LLM gives instructions:
**** Required documents
**** Website links
**** Step-by-step process
*** You must perform all steps yourself.

** Using an Agent
*** Similar to a real-world passport agent.
*** The agent:
**** Collects your documents
**** Fills forms
**** Books appointments
**** Guides you through submission
*** The agent performs tasks on your behalf.

* Core Idea of AI Agents
** AI agents are not separate from LLMs.
** The LLM is still the core brain.
** Additional capabilities are added through tools.

* Role of Tools in AI Agents
** Tools give agents the ability to act.
** Examples of tools:
*** Database connectors
*** Browser automation
*** API integrations
*** Code editing tools
*** Reporting or visualization tools

** Without tools:
*** The system behaves like a normal LLM.
*** It only knows information but cannot act.

* Brain and Hands Analogy
** LLM = Brain
*** Understands problems
*** Generates reasoning and solutions

** Tools = Hands
*** Execute actions
*** Interact with systems
*** Modify data or code

** #AI Agent = Brain + Hands#
*** Understands tasks
*** Decides actions
*** Executes them automatically

* Examples of AI Agent Tasks
** Book a flight to Paris
*** Opens browser
*** Navigates to travel websites
*** Searches flights
*** Books tickets

** Fix a bug in a repository
*** Reads codebase
*** Identifies faulty lines
*** Edits files
*** Writes corrected code

** Generate monthly sales report
*** Connects to database
*** Queries sales data
*** Processes results
*** Generates charts or reports

* Decision Making Capability
** Agents can take autonomous decisions.
** They analyze the environment and choose actions.
** They execute multiple steps to achieve a goal.

* Definition of an AI Agent
** An AI agent is an autonomous system that:
*** Understands user goals
*** Takes decisions
*** Uses tools
*** Performs actions
*** Produces results automatically

* Importance of Tool Integration
** Tools are what make agents powerful.
** They extend LLM capability into real-world systems.
** Proper tool connection is required to activate agent abilities.

* Next Concept: MCP (Model Context Protocol)
** MCP provides a standardized way to connect tools to AI agents.
** It enables agents to interact with systems and perform actions.
** Understanding MCP is essential before building AI agents.

* Learning Path
** Understand LLM fundamentals
** Understand AI agent architecture
** Learn how tools integrate with agents
** Learn MCP
** Build single agents
** Build multi-agent systems

=== 5. What is MCP? How this MCP help an LLM to be super powerful

* Building an AI Agent Using MCP Servers
** Goal is to convert plain English instructions into automated business workflows
** Automation includes browser actions, database queries, API calls, and file operations
** Achieved by combining Large Language Models with MCP servers

* Example Automation Workflow
** User provides plain English prompt describing the task
** Agent performs end-to-end automation without writing code
*** Query database for registration data
*** Fill registration form in web browser
*** Call REST API to verify registration
*** Store results in Excel file
*** Complete login automation

* Role of Large Language Models (LLMs)
** LLMs process user prompts and understand intent
** They generate reasoning and decide which actions are needed
** However, LLMs cannot directly access systems
*** Cannot query databases
*** Cannot automate browsers
*** Cannot call APIs
*** Cannot access local files

* Need for MCP (Model Context Protocol)
** MCP connects LLMs with external systems and tools
** Provides context that LLMs normally lack
** Enables LLMs to interact with real-world resources

* Definition of MCP
** Open protocol that standardizes how applications provide context to LLMs
** Enables AI systems to interact with external tools and data sources
** Works similarly to a universal connector like USB-C for AI systems

* Context Problem Without MCP
** LLMs cannot answer questions about private data
*** Example: "Total number of orders in my database"
** Reason: LLM has no access to internal systems
** MCP bridges this gap by connecting LLMs to resources

* MCP Architecture
** MCP Client
*** Interface used by users (e.g., Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini)
** MCP Server
*** Middle layer connecting LLMs to external systems
** External Resources
*** Databases
*** Browsers
*** File systems
*** APIs

* How MCP Works
** User asks a question to the LLM
** LLM interprets the intent using natural language understanding
** LLM communicates with MCP server using standardized protocol
** MCP server calls tool functions
** Tool functions interact with external systems
** Results are returned to the LLM and presented to the user

* Tool Functions in MCP Servers
** MCP servers expose tools (functions) that LLMs can use
** Each tool performs a specific operation
*** Execute SQL query
*** Navigate browser
*** Click elements
*** Read files
*** Call APIs
** LLM selects the correct tool based on description

* Example: Database Query with MCP
** LLM receives question about customer orders
** LLM checks available MCP tools
** It finds tool "execute_sql"
** LLM first inspects database structure
*** Tables
*** Columns
*** Relationships
** LLM generates appropriate SQL query
** MCP executes query and returns results

* Example: Browser Automation with MCP
** Browser automation MCP server exposes functions
*** browser_navigate
*** browser_click
*** browser_type
*** browser_go_back
** LLM chooses functions based on user instruction
*** Example prompt: "Go to a website and run smoke test"
** MCP executes browser automation steps

* MCP Server Configuration
** MCP servers are connected using simple configuration
** Typically done through a JSON configuration file
** Only a few lines are required
** Once configured, LLM automatically detects available tools

* Multiple MCP Servers Can Be Connected
** Database MCP server
** Browser automation MCP server
** File system MCP server
** API testing MCP server
** Excel manipulation MCP server

* Key Capability Enabled by MCP
** Plain English instructions trigger complex workflows
** AI agent decides which tools to use
** Actions executed automatically across systems

* AI Agent Architecture
** LLM = reasoning engine (brain)
** MCP tools = execution capabilities (hands)
** Together they create a functional AI agent

* Practical Example Workflow
** Retrieve user data from database
** Fill web registration form
** Verify success through API
** Store results in Excel
** Perform browser automation tests

* Key Insight
** AI agents are created by combining
*** LLM reasoning
*** MCP tool integrations
** LLM decides what to do
** MCP tools perform the actual actions

* Future Learning Focus
** Understanding MCP concepts deeply
** Learning how MCP tools connect to agents
** Building multi-agent systems using MCP
** Creating automated workflows using AI agents

== Section 3

=== 12. Disadvantages of having Single Agent - and how Multi Agent fixes the problem

* A single AI agent can use multiple MCP tools to perform different tasks
  ** The large language model acts as the brain of the agent
  ** The agent can handle browser automation, database queries, API calls, and file system access

* Single-agent architecture creates too many responsibilities for one agent
  ** One agent must manage many different capabilities
  *** Browser automation
  *** Database access
  *** API communication
  *** File handling

* This creates a “jack of all trades, master of none” situation
  ** The agent can do many tasks
  ** But it may not perform any single task optimally

* Tool complexity increases in a single-agent system
  ** One agent may have many tools connected
  ** Example comparison
  *** One agent with 20 tools
  *** Four agents with 5 tools each
  ** Managing fewer tools per agent is simpler

* Single-agent systems become hard to manage and debug
  ** Similar to writing an entire application in one file
  ** Similar to placing 100 automation test cases in one test file

* *Tasks in a single-agent system usually run sequentially*
  ** One task must finish before the next begins
  ** This creates bottlenecks and delays

* Multi-agent systems solve these problems
  ** Instead of one general agent, multiple specialized agents are created

* Each agent focuses on a specific capability
  ** Database agent
  *** Responsible for database queries and data retrieval
  ** API agent
  *** Handles API communication
  ** Excel or file agent
  *** Manages spreadsheets or file operations

* Specialized agents improve results
  ** Each agent becomes an expert in its domain
  ** Coordination between agents leads to better outcomes

* *Multi-agent systems allow parallel processing*
  ** Different agents can work simultaneously
  *** One agent gathers data
  *** Another agent processes the data

* Multi-agent systems are easier to maintain
  ** Changes in one agent do not affect others
  ** Example
  *** Updating API agent prompts does not affect the database agent

* Multi-agent systems improve scalability
  ** New capabilities can be added by introducing new agents
  ** Agents can be removed if not needed

* Multi-agent systems help control cost
  ** Each agent uses the LLM brain and consumes tokens
  ** Removing unnecessary agents reduces token usage and cost

* Multi-agent architecture is preferred for structured workflows
  ** Specialized agents collaborate
  ** Each agent performs a specific task in the overall system

=== 13. What is Agentic AI? Importance of Multi Agent work flows


* Multi-agent systems can follow different workflow patterns
  ** Each workflow defines how agents collaborate to complete tasks

* *Sequential workflow*
  ** Tasks are executed step by step by different agents
  ** Output of one agent becomes input to the next agent
  ** Example workflow
  *** Agent A collects data
  *** Agent B analyzes the data
  *** Agent C generates a report

* *Parallel workflow*
  ** Multiple agents work simultaneously if tasks are independent
  ** This improves performance and reduces waiting time
  ** Example workflow
  *** Agent A performs API testing
  *** Agent D performs another independent task
  *** Agent C starts database analysis after Agent B finishes

* *Hierarchical workflow*
  ** A manager agent controls other agents
  ** The manager decides which agent should perform a task
  ** Agents are invoked dynamically at runtime
  ** The user only provides the goal
  ** The manager agent orchestrates the workflow automatically

* *Collaborative workflow*
  ** Agents communicate with each other continuously
  ** Communication happens in a round-robin manner
  ** Agents share intermediate results and improve decisions together

* *AI agents can collaborate to achieve a common goal*
  ** Each agent performs a specific responsibility
  ** Agents coordinate through workflows

* *Definition of Agentic AI*
  ** Agentic AI refers to systems that autonomously take actions and make decisions to achieve goals
  ** The system goes beyond simply responding to prompts

* #*Difference between AI agents and Agentic AI*#
  ** AI agent
  *** An individual autonomous system with tools and reasoning
  ** Agentic AI
  *** The entire process where multiple AI agents collaborate to solve a problem

* Agentic AI focuses on the process of collaboration
  ** Multiple agents work together through workflows
  ** The system coordinates actions to achieve the final goal

* Agentic AI systems are autonomous
  ** They make decisions without constant human guidance
  ** They initiate actions proactively instead of only reacting to prompts

* Orchestration is an important concept in Agentic AI
  ** It defines how multiple agents coordinate with each other
  ** Example orchestration patterns
  *** Sequential workflows
  *** Hierarchical workflows
  *** Collaborative workflows

* Reasoning happens at each step of the workflow
  ** Each agent analyzes information before performing actions
  ** This improves decision quality

* Traditional LLM systems vs Agentic AI

** Traditional LLM systems
*** Focus on answering questions
*** Usually involve a single interaction
*** Mostly reactive

** Agentic AI systems
*** Focus on solving problems
*** Involve multi-step interactions
*** Are proactive and goal driven
*** Can take autonomous decisions

* Tools play a critical role in AI agents
  ** LLM alone acts as the brain
  ** Tools provide the capability to interact with external systems

* MCP tools enable AI agents to perform real-world actions
  ** Example capabilities
  *** Browser automation
  *** Database access
  *** API interactions
  *** File system operations

* #*Core architecture of Agentic AI*#

** Large Language Model
*** Acts as the brain for reasoning and decision making

** Tools (such as MCP)
*** Provide the ability to perform actions

** AI Agents
*** Combine the brain and tools to execute tasks

** Multi-Agent System
*** Multiple agents collaborate through workflows

** Agentic AI
*** The complete system where multiple agents coordinate to achieve a goal
