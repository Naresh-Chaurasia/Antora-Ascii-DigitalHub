---

Agentic frameworks simplify building complex multi-agent systems by handling communication, coordination, and infrastructure

* Learning Objectives
** Understand challenges of building multi-agent systems from scratch
** Identify how frameworks simplify agent development
** Compare key agentic frameworks and their strengths
** Recognize ideal use cases for each framework
** Apply the right framework based on system needs

---

* *Challenges Without Frameworks*
** *Communication complexity*
*** Need to manage message protocols manually
** *State management*
*** Sync data across multiple agents manually
** *Coordination logic*
*** Write custom workflows for agent interaction
** *Error handling*
*** Handle failures across distributed agents
*** *Monitoring and debugging*
*** Difficult to trace issues in multi-agent systems

---

* *Role of Frameworks*
** Simplification
** Built-in communication protocols
** Automatic state management
** Predefined coordination patterns
** Standardized error handling
** Integrated monitoring and debugging tools

---

* *CrewAI*
** Concept
*** Simulates team-based collaboration
** Key features
*** Define agents with roles (researcher, writer, editor)
*** Assign tasks to each agent
*** Structured outputs for coordination
** Example
*** Writing a blog with multiple agents
** Best use cases
*** Content creation
*** Collaborative workflows
*** Rapid prototyping

---

* *LangGraph*
** Concept
*** Workflow control using directed graphs
** Key features
*** Nodes represent steps (LLM call, tool, decision)
*** Edges define transitions and outcomes
*** Fine-grained control over execution
** Example
*** Flowchart-like agent workflow
** Best use cases
*** Multi-step processes
*** Document workflows
*** Decision trees

---

* *AutoGen (AG2)*
** Concept
*** Conversation-driven agent collaboration
** Key features
*** Agent-to-agent and human interaction
*** Real-time problem solving
*** Supports human-in-the-loop
*** Can execute code within conversations
** Example
*** Interactive coding assistant
** Best use cases
*** Education
*** Technical support
*** Collaborative coding

---

* *Pydantic AI*
** Concept
*** Structured and type-safe AI outputs
** Key features
*** Schema validation
*** Reduces ambiguity and errors
*** Modular design
** Example
*** API response validation
** Best use cases
*** Enterprise systems
*** APIs and structured data
*** Production environments

---

* *BeeAI*
** Concept
*** Flexible and enterprise-ready agent system
** Key features
*** Custom agent behavior design
*** Integration with multiple tools (LLMs, APIs)
*** Memory and state persistence
*** Telemetry and logging
*** Failure handling with exceptions
** Example
*** Enterprise automation workflows
** Best use cases
*** Scalable production systems
*** Enterprise automation
*** Complex integrations

---

* *Framework Comparison Insight*
** Easy to start
*** CrewAI and AutoGen
**** Best for beginners and rapid prototyping
** High control
*** LangGraph and Pydantic AI
**** Suitable for structured workflows and reliability
** Enterprise scale
*** BeeAI
**** Designed for robustness and production systems

---

* *Real-World Applications*
** CrewAI
*** Content pipelines and reporting
** LangGraph
*** Document workflows and customer service
** AutoGen
*** Educational platforms and support systems
** Pydantic AI
*** API validation and structured outputs
** BeeAI
*** Enterprise automation systems

* Quick Revision Points
** Multi-agent systems are complex without frameworks
** Frameworks handle communication, state, and coordination
** CrewAI is best for role-based collaboration
** LangGraph is ideal for structured workflows
** AutoGen excels in conversational systems
** Pydantic AI ensures structured, reliable outputs
** BeeAI is built for enterprise-grade scalability
** Choose framework based on simplicity, control, or scale
